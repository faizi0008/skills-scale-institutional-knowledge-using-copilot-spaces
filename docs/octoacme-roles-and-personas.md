# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define and coordinate testing strategies, ensuring products meet quality standards before release. They bridge development, product, and project management to maintain high quality throughout the delivery lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define comprehensive testing strategies and quality standards
- Coordinate test planning, execution, and defect management
- Collaborate with developers on test automation and coverage
- Communicate quality metrics and release readiness to stakeholders
- Establish and maintain quality gates for releases
- Mentor team members on testing best practices

### Goals
- Ensure product quality meets defined standards
- Minimize post-release defects and customer-impacting issues
- Improve test automation coverage and efficiency
- Provide clear visibility into quality metrics

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup participation and sprint planning
- Test plan reviews and quality gate checkpoints
- Release readiness reports to Project Managers
- Quality metrics dashboards and defect reviews

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Work closely on test planning, automation, and defect resolution. Review unit test coverage and integration test scenarios.
- **Product Managers**: Validate that acceptance criteria are testable and that quality standards align with customer expectations.
- **Project Managers**: Provide release readiness assessments, communicate quality risks, and coordinate testing timelines.

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee change requests, assess impacts, and coordinate communication strategies. They ensure changes are evaluated, approved, and implemented smoothly with minimal disruption to ongoing work.

### Responsibilities
- Manage change request intake and evaluation process
- Conduct impact assessments for proposed changes
- Coordinate change approval workflows
- Communicate change implications to affected teams and stakeholders
- Track change implementation and outcomes
- Maintain change logs and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure changes are properly evaluated and approved
- Minimize disruption from unplanned or poorly coordinated changes
- Maintain clear audit trails for all changes
- Balance agility with stability and control

### Typical Communication
- Change review board meetings
- Impact assessment reports
- Change notifications and status updates
- Post-implementation reviews

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Collaborate on change approval decisions and timeline impacts. Coordinate resource allocation for change implementation.
- **Developers**: Work with development teams to understand technical impacts and implementation approaches.
- **Stakeholder Engagement Specialists**: Partner on communicating changes to stakeholders and gathering feedback on change proposals.

---

## Stakeholder Engagement Specialist

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Engagement Specialists coordinate communication and feedback with internal and external stakeholders. They ensure stakeholder needs and concerns are addressed throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map key stakeholders and their interests
- Develop and execute stakeholder communication plans
- Gather and synthesize stakeholder feedback
- Facilitate stakeholder meetings and presentations
- Manage stakeholder expectations and concerns
- Ensure stakeholder requirements are captured and addressed

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder relationships and trust
- Ensure stakeholder needs are understood and represented
- Proactively manage stakeholder expectations
- Foster transparent, consistent communication

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder updates and newsletters
- Stakeholder feedback sessions and surveys
- Executive briefings and presentations
- Cross-functional collaboration meetings

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share stakeholder insights to inform product decisions and priorities. Validate that roadmap aligns with stakeholder needs.
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on stakeholder communication plans and timing. Provide input on stakeholder-related risks.
- **Change Managers**: Partner on communicating changes and gathering stakeholder input on change proposals.

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches support teams in adopting and improving agile methodologies. They mentor team members, facilitate agile ceremonies, help remove impediments, and promote continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Guide teams in agile practices and principles
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, planning)
- Coach team members on collaboration and self-organization
- Identify and help remove impediments
- Promote continuous improvement and learning
- Advocate for agile best practices across the organization

### Goals
- Enable teams to work effectively using agile methodologies
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Increase team velocity and predictability
- Build self-organizing, high-performing teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily participation in agile ceremonies
- One-on-one coaching sessions
- Retrospective facilitation and action planning
- Cross-team practice sharing and learning sessions

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coach on technical practices like TDD, pair programming, and continuous integration. Help resolve team dynamics issues.
- **Product Managers**: Guide on effective backlog management, user story writing, and prioritization techniques.
- **Project Managers**: Partner on process improvements, planning approaches, and removing organizational impediments.
- **All Roles**: Work across all roles to foster agile mindset, facilitate collaboration, and drive continuous improvement.
